Who I Help

Therapy is tailored to the person or family in front of me. Here are some of the people I most often support, and the concerns we can navigate together.

Children & Preteens (5+)

For kids, therapy is playful, creative, and grounded in safety. We might talk, draw, move, or use sensory tools to help them express what’s going on inside.

  • Big feelings, meltdowns, or “out of nowhere” reactions
  • School or peer struggles
  • Grief, loss, or scary experiences
  • Adoption, foster care, and family transitions

Teens & Young Adults

Many teens and young adults show up carrying anxiety, people-pleasing, or a loud inner critic. We work together to untangle where those patterns started and what you want next.

  • Anxiety, depression, and burnout
  • Identity, relationships, and family conflict
  • Trauma and PTSD
  • School, work, and life transitions

Parents, Caregivers & Families

Parenting is hard; parenting kids with trauma, neurodivergence, or complex behavior can feel impossible. We focus on connection, practical tools, and care for you as a person.

  • Adoption and post-adoption support
  • Behavioral and attachment challenges
  • Co-parenting and family communication
  • Support for caregivers who are exhausted and overwhelmed

Inclusive, affirming care

My practice is affirming for LGBTQ+ folks, diverse family structures, non-monogamous relationships, and people navigating faith shifts and cultural questions.

Therapy is a shame-free zone. You don’t have to earn your way into being taken seriously here.
