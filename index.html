About Mountains to Sea Therapy

Hi, I’m Abby Teed (she/her), LCSW, a clinical social worker offering online therapy for clients across North Carolina. I’m originally from Minnesota and have lived in both the mountains and the coast of North Carolina, which deeply shapes how I think about change, seasons, and healing. I’ve spent over a decade walking alongside children, teens, and families through foster care, adoption, in-home services, and outpatient therapy.

My work centers on helping people who have lived through hard things—big and small—and who are ready to stop pretending everything is fine. Together we make room for the messy, human parts of your story with warmth, humor, and a lot of compassion.

Whether you’re a kid feeling misunderstood, a parent feeling stuck, or an adult trying to untangle old patterns, our time is a space where you don’t have to perform. You get to be real, in your own way, at your own pace.

Why “Mountains to Sea”?

The name Mountains to Sea Therapy holds pieces of my own story as well as the kind of work I do with clients. I grew up in Minnesota, surrounded by lakes and long winters, and later found myself moving between the mountains of North Carolina and, eventually, the coast.

Those landscapes feel a lot like the inner worlds my clients describe. The mountains remind me of the slow, steady work of healing—showing up, one step at a time, even when the path is steep and you can’t see the view yet. The sea feels more like the nervous system: tides that rise and fall, sudden storms, moments of calm, and waves that keep returning to the shoreline.

Mountains to Sea is my shorthand for the whole range of being human—from the uphill climbs to the days you’re just trying to stay afloat. All of it belongs, and none of it disqualifies you from care.

My hope is that this practice feels like both a trailhead and a shoreline: a place where you can pause, get your bearings, and choose what comes next with support, instead of having to figure it all out alone.

How I Work

I blend several trauma-informed approaches so we can work with both your mind and your body. Sessions are relaxed, collaborative, and tailored to what you need that day rather than a rigid script.

You might notice we check in with your body—your breath, your muscles, your posture—as much as your thoughts. We might use movement, tapping, or grounding exercises to help your nervous system feel safer while we process hard things.

Some sessions are deep and emotional. Others are about problem-solving, parenting strategies, or just having one hour where you don’t have to hold everything together. All of it counts.

Modalities I draw from

  • EMDR – to process traumatic or overwhelming experiences
  • Somatic Therapy – noticing how stress and memory live in the body
  • Internal Family Systems / Parts Work – understanding the different “parts” of you
  • Attachment-based & Family Systems – focusing on relationships and connection
  • Playful, creative approaches – especially with kids & teens

We’ll always talk about what we’re doing and why, and you’re encouraged to say what is or isn’t working for you.
